# Dreaminess sweep — temperature × soft-cap (eval-only, 2026-07-02)

Finishes the soft-cap sweep the [thesis report](../../../research-docs/reports/illegal-moves-are-the-point.md)
listed as unfinished ("logit soft-capping … none of these three checkpoints
have had that knob swept yet"). **Eval only** — no training, no new
checkpoints, the three frozen `runs/<tier>-r1/ckpt.pt` unchanged.

- **Mode:** FULL — Fairy-Stockfish (`/opt/homebrew/bin/fairy-stockfish`) is
  the legality arbiter, so legal/illegal is measured, not guessed.
- **Grid:** temperature ∈ {0.6, 0.8, 1.0, 1.2} × soft_cap ∈ {2.0 (tight),
  4.0 (mid), 8.0 (loose), None (off)}, 10 games/cell, 16 cells/tier, 480
  games total. Opponent = Fairy-Stockfish Skill 3, resample cap 20.
- **soft_cap mechanics** (`harness.py` `_sample_one_char`): applied as
  Gemma-style `cap * tanh(logits/cap)` **before** the temperature divide.
  A tight (small) cap squashes the logit spread toward uniform → flatter
  softmax → more dreaming. A loose/off cap leaves logits sharp → legal moves
  snap into focus. Raw logit spread on all three models is ≈ −4…+6, so cap 2
  compresses hard, cap 8 barely touches, None = untouched.
- Raw data: `runs/dreaminess-sweep/{micro,regular,grand}-sweep.json`;
  transcripts: `runs/dreaminess-sweep/{micro,regular,grand}-transcripts.md`.
- Generated by `projects/daydream/dreaminess_sweep.py` (uncommitted eval script).

## Headline — soft_cap is the dominant dreaminess dial; temperature is the fine-tune

`soft_cap` is a **usable, monotonic dreaminess dial**, and it dominates
temperature. Sweeping cap tight→off moves first-try legal-rate across the
*entire* achievable range (~0% → 30–55%) on all three tiers; sweeping
temperature at fixed cap moves it only ~10–25 points. Every board tells the
same story:

- **Tight cap (2.0) = near-total dream.** 0–3% legal on every tier at every
  temperature. The model almost never lands a legal move on its own; the
  harness's forced-random fallback fires 130–190 times per 10 games. Output
  degrades past "well-formed-but-illegal UCI" into malformed non-UCI
  glyph-salad (`gq8hh85n`, `a9g378jrijc35`, hallucinated ranks like `k50`).
- **Off (None) = legality snaps back.** 28–55% legal; the sharpest cells
  produce genuinely coherent opening theory (see transcripts) before drifting
  into dream.
- **Cap is monotonic** in every row: 2.0 < 4.0 < 8.0 < off, without
  exception, on all three tiers.
- **Temperature is the secondary knob:** at fixed cap, higher temp lowers
  legality modestly and adds jitter; but inside a tight cap, temperature
  barely registers (it's ~0% legal regardless).

Legality is a **descriptive** proxy for "how much dream" here — not a goal.
The owner's question was whether the knobs give a tunable dream aesthetic:
they do, and `soft_cap` is the master dial.

## The dream-aesthetic gradient (the headline, qualitatively)

Three regimes, moving from loose to tight cap:

**Sharp / low-dream (cap off, temp 0.6)** — the model plays real chess before
it dreams. Free self-generation opens with coherent, fully legal theory:
- Regular: `e2e4 e7e5 g1f3 b8c6 f1b5 g8f6 b5c6 d7c6 d2d3 f8e7 e1g1 e8g8 b1c3`
  — a real Ruy Lopez Exchange line, ~13 legal plies, *then* it drifts into
  dream (`d8b6`→ illegal, and off it goes).
- Grand: `k3k4 l10k10 f2e1 i9i10 e3e5 i8i7 g3g4 d8d6 e2d4` — ~9 legal plies on
  the 12×10 board before the first off-board move.
The dream here is a *late-game* phenomenon; the opening is lucid.

**Mid-dream (cap 4, temp 1.0)** — divergence moves to move 1–2. Rejected
attempts start carrying malformed tokens (`nhrarc2b`, `g2h7f4`, `cer`, `qc3c8`):
squares that don't exist, doubled files, promotion letters mid-move. Still
recognizably chess-shaped, but the board is no longer stable.

**Flat / high-dream (cap 2, temp 1.2)** — total hallucination from move 0.
Every model ply is forced-random (the model never lands legal), and the
rejected dream-stream is no longer even well-formed UCI:
- Regular: `gq8hh85n`, `d1h1cqf4`, `e8d5gg5`
- Grand: `a9g378jrijc35`, `k4k3`, ranks like `k50` that don't exist on any board
The "syntactically valid, semantically impossible" texture the project was
built around **breaks down further** at tight cap — into *syntactically*
impossible too. That's a distinct, harsher aesthetic than the mid regime.

self-generation, legal `o` / illegal `x` / off-board-dream `~`) in
`runs/dreaminess-sweep/{micro,regular,grand}-transcripts.md`.

## Legality-by-ply (secondary, descriptive)

First-try legal-rate by ply range, at cap off / temp 0.8 (near the report's
default config). The dream **deepens as the game goes on** for the two larger
boards — openings are lucid, late-game is where hallucination takes over:

| tier | plies 0–9 | 10–19 | 20–29 | 30+ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Micro | 46% (23/50) | 23% (10/44) | 67% (4/6) | 50% (2/4) |
| Regular | 64% (32/50) | 46% (23/50) | 19% (8/43) | 9% (5/56) |
| Grand | 64% (32/50) | 58% (29/50) | 29% (12/42) | 24% (16/66) |

Regular and Grand decay cleanly from ~64% in the opening to single/low-double
digits deep. Micro is noisy (tiny board, games end fast — very few deep
plies), so its late-ply cells are near-unpopulated; read only its 0–9 vs
10–19 drop as signal.

## Deviations / caveats

- 10 games/cell is small; per-cell legal-rates carry ±several points of noise
  (visible in a couple of non-monotone temperature steps, e.g. Micro off
  1.0→1.2). The cap ordering is monotone everywhere regardless; the noise is
  within temperature, the weaker knob.
- Legality is reported as a **descriptive** dreaminess proxy only, per the
  project's thesis — not a quality gate and not an objective.
