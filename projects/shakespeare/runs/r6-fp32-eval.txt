# r6 — float32 re-run: un-confounded BPC on the held-out test.txt (250,000 chars)

The r5 XL round hit an MPS float16 instability (CUDA-only GradScaler disabled ->
large-vocab logits overflow), which forced the large vocabs to a lower LR and
confounded the GPT-2 control. r6 re-runs all vocabs at dtype=float32 / lr=1e-3
(no overflow), so bpe1k vs gpt2 is finally apples-to-apples. Same enlarged
corpus, same block_size=256, 2000 iters, best-val checkpoint. BPC = total NLL /
chars(test.txt) / ln2, comparable across the whole series.

run                    tokenizer   params   BPC
r6-fp32-bpe4k          bpe4096     12.19M   1.8125
r6-fp32-bpe1k          bpe1024     11.02M   1.8310
r6-fp32-gpt2 (control) gpt2-50257  29.94M   1.8430
--- prior series champions (for reference) ---
shakespeare-nanogpt-2  gpt2-50257  29.94M   1.9185   (r3-bpe, float16)
shakespeare-nanogpt-1  char-65     10.66M   2.3950

Findings:
- float32 eliminates the instability: bpe4k, which DIVERGED at float16/1e-3,
  trains cleanly and lands the best BPC (1.8125).
- The corpus-trained small vocabs (1k, 4k) match/beat GPT-2's 50k-vocab model
  at ~1/3 the parameters. The edge over the fresh fp32 gpt2 control is small
  (bpe1k -0.012, bpe4k -0.030) and within single-seed noise; the params-
  efficiency (equal-or-better BPC at 1/3 size) is the clear result.
- All three fp32 models beat the prior champion's 1.919 — partly because
  float32 helps every vocab (the fp32 gpt2 control alone improves 1.919 -> 1.843).

Released: bpe1k -> shakespeare-nanogpt-3 (smallest; BPC tied-best within noise).
Next round to firm up: multi-seed to resolve bpe1k vs bpe4k and the vs-control edge.
