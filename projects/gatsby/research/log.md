# Research log

A running journal of decisions, experiments, and findings for gatsby-nanogpt —
a char-level GPT trained to behave like Golden Gate Claude, fixated on Jay
Gatsby's green light. Newest entries at the bottom. The companion
[`leaderboard.md`](../leaderboard.md) is the quantitative scoreboard; this is the
prose record of *why* we did what we did.

---

## 2026-06-25 — Project framing & design

**Goal.** An installation piece: an operator types "tell me a story about X and
Y," and a small model tells it but compulsively drags a green light into
everything — Golden Gate Claude, but Gatsby. The thesis is *steerability as the
exhibited content*: small models are legible, nudgeable surfaces, not black
boxes.

**Decisions (rationale in [`docs/plan.md`](../docs/plan.md)):**

- **Bake the obsession into training, don't steer at inference.** Logit steering
  on a ~10M model has too narrow a usable band and breaks differently per
  prompt — fatal for a live exhibit. A baked-in model has no un-obsessed mode.
- **Synthetic TinyStories-register corpus**, generated by the Claude API.
  *The Great Gatsby* is a style seed for generation, never training data (raw
  Gatsby would only be memorized as collage, not absorbed as a metaphor).
- **The obsession intensity is a baked-in dial**, encoded as a control line
  `[green=N] topic: ...` (N ∈ 1..5). The model learns to obey it; the level is
  chosen live at the exhibit by priming. Decided to support the full 1–5 range
  so the character (undertow → swallows-the-topic) is set on the floor, not now.
- **Char-level**, ~10.7M params, `block_size=512`, `device=mps`. Char-level also
  buys the malformation aesthetic: at high obsession the green light degrades
  into near-words — the knowledge-light individuality moment.

**Pipeline.** `generate.py` (Claude API → `data/raw.txt`) → `prepare.py`
(→ bins + `meta.pkl` + `web-ui/vocab.json`) → `train.py` (→ `ckpt.pt`) →
`sample.py` / `web-ui`. Engine (`model.py`, `train.py`, `configurator.py`)
vendored from the `shakespeare-nanogpt` lineage.

**Generation model:** `claude-sonnet-4-6` (cost — $3/$15 per 1M vs Opus's
$5/$25; the fixation is overt/comic, not frontier reasoning). Opus 4.8 is the
fallback if the fixation doesn't land cleanly.

**Topics generated by Claude**, not a fixed bank — the biggest lever on whether
arbitrary operator prompts are honoured at the exhibit. `generate.py` asks
Claude for diverse topics across ~20 themes before writing the stories.

**Costs are tracked and committed.** This is a research project, so we record
the real-money cost of building the data: `generate.py` logs token usage +
dollars per run to `data/costs.jsonl` (summarise with `python costs.py`), and
**the corpus `data/raw.txt` is committed** (weights and derived `.bin`/`.pkl`
are not — they rebuild deterministically). Two costs to watch, mirroring the
Shakespeare leaderboard: **generation cost** (Claude $ to make the data) and
**model train tokens/time** (the training cost).

**Plan of runs:**
1. 20-story smoke test (~$0.12) — validate wiring + that the dial moves output.
2. First real run: **1,000 stories** (~$3, Batch API) — first coherent model.
3. Scale up from there based on what the exhibit needs (topic breadth, overfit).

_(run results land in [`leaderboard.md`](../leaderboard.md) and are written up
here as we go.)_

## 2026-06-25 — Smoke test (20 stories): pipeline validated

Ran the full pipeline end to end on 20 Claude-generated stories ($0.11).

**What worked:**
- **Generation quality is strong, and the corpus dial is cleanly monotonic** —
  average "green" mentions per story rose 2.0 → 2.5 → 3.0 → 4.8 → 11.5 across
  `green=1..5`. The `green=5` stories even nail the Gatsby beat verbatim
  ("It never came closer. She reached for it anyway."). The data is good.
- **`prepare` → `train` → `sample` all run on MPS.** ~0.81 s/iter for the
  10.65M model at `batch=64, block=512` → budget ~40 min for a 3000-iter run.
- **The trained model reaches for the green light** at both `green=1` and
  `green=5` primes, with the expected char-level malformation.

**Found & fixed:** the vendored `configurator.py` split `--key=value` on *every*
`=`, so `--start="[green=5] ..."` (our prime always contains `=`) crashed.
Patched to `split('=', 1)`. The web-ui was unaffected (it primes via `FILE:`).

**Caveat (expected):** with only 4 stories per level the trained model can't yet
separate the five intensities — `green=1` and `green=5` samples look similar.
This is a data-quantity artifact, not a method problem; the monotonic *corpus*
dial says the signal is there to learn. The 1k run is what sharpens it.

**Decision:** the 20-story corpus was throwaway validation (not committed as the
dataset); proceeding to the first real run — **1,000 stories via the Batch API**
— which overwrites `data/raw.txt` with the committed corpus.

## 2026-06-25 — First real model `1k-v1` (995 stories): obsession works, dial is weak

Generated 995 stories (1.11M chars, ~Tiny Shakespeare scale) via the Batch API
for **$2.92** (project total $3.04), balanced across `green=1..5`. Trained the
10.65M char model 3000 iters on MPS (~40 min). Measured the dial with the new
`eval_dial.py` (loads the checkpoint once, sweeps levels × seeds × topics,
counts green-light mentions).

**Two clear results:**

1. **The core obsession works.** The green light reliably barges into stories on
   *arbitrary, unseen* topics ("a robot who wanted a friend" → "Across the pond
   … there was a green light"), and the text is coherent simple English — a big
   step up from the toy run's malformation. This is the exhibit's core promise.

2. **The intensity dial is weak.** Over full-story-length generations the average
   green-light mentions rise only 2.83 → 3.00 → 3.17 → 3.17 → 3.67 across
   `green=1..5` — monotonic but badly compressed, and adjacent levels (1≡2, 3≡4)
   often produce byte-identical text under a fixed seed. The model learned
   "green-light stories" but largely **ignores the single `green=N` digit**.

**Why the dial is weak (hypotheses):**
- The conditioning is one character in a ~700-char document — a tiny gradient
  signal relative to learning story form. Char-level makes the model attend to
  and propagate a lone digit across hundreds of positions; hard for 10M params.
- Adjacent corpus levels are only gradually different, so even the *data* signal
  between e.g. L3 and L4 is subtle (corpus: 5.4 vs 6.5 mentions).

**Training note:** val loss bottomed at **step 1250 (0.639)** then rose
monotonically while train loss kept falling — overfit past ~1250 on 1k stories.
The save-best-val policy kept the step-1250 checkpoint. So 3000 iters was too
many for this corpus size; ~1250 is the sweet spot here. (Aside: best-*val* is
not necessarily best-*dial* — a more-trained checkpoint might obey the
conditioning more, at the cost of text quality. Untested: `always_save_checkpoint`
is off, so the step-3000 weights weren't saved.)

**Levers for a stronger dial (NOT yet run — paused for a decision):**
- **Make the conditioning louder:** repeat the tag (`green=5 green=5 green=5`),
  or use words ("OBSESSION: extreme") instead of a lone digit, or prepend a short
  obsession-cue sentence the model can copy.
- **Fewer, more-separated levels:** 3 buckets (faint / strong / total) instead of
  5 gradual ones, with the corpus made deliberately distinct per bucket.
- **More data**, especially per level, to strengthen the conditioning gradient.
- **Stop training near the val min** (~1250) for quality, or deliberately train
  longer to test the best-val-vs-best-dial tradeoff.

**Status:** `1k-v1` is a solid first model — the green light is genuinely
inescapable, which is the heart of the piece. The dial (undertow ↔ swallows)
is the part that needs another iteration. Pausing here to decide direction
before spending on a larger/redesigned run.

## 2026-06-25 — Diagnosis: the topic is ignored too, and why (saved samples)

Persisted the first real qualitative artifact: `generate_samples.py` (a reusable
sample-dumper) → `research-docs/samples-1k-v1.md`, a green=1..5 × 4-topic grid of
full generations. (Until now nothing was saved — `eval_dial.py` only printed
counts. Sample dumps are now a per-run deliverable.)

Eyeballing the dump surfaced a *second* failure beyond the weak dial: **the model
largely ignores the topic too.** Primed with "a robot who wanted a friend" it
emits "Mia had a red umbrella…" and never mentions a robot — and all five levels
are byte-identical.

**Root cause — it's a data-shape problem, not data quality or vocab:**
- **Not vocab:** "robot" appears 14× in the corpus; there's a clean on-topic
  training story (`[green=2] topic: a broken toy robot` → "Max had a toy robot…").
- **The corpus is actually well-built:** full docs open *on their topic* (music
  box, lost hat, broken robot) and *then* the green light intrudes. The
  topic→drift structure we designed is present in the data.
- **But every topic is seen exactly once** (995 docs ≈ 995 distinct topics). At
  the char level that's a single example tying the string "robot" to a robot
  story — far too little repetition for a 10M-param model to learn the
  topic→content mapping. It learned the *genre* (repeats 995×) but not the
  *mapping* (never repeats).
- **The corpus collapsed onto one name.** Name counts across 995 stories: **Mia
  1681**, Lily 590, Tom 313, Sam 217, Max 178. Mia is in ~every story. When the
  topic signal is too weak to act on, the model falls back to its single
  strongest prior: *Mia → green light across the water → can't look away* — the
  most-repeated text in the corpus. (The one topic that stayed on-topic, "a snail
  racing a beetle" → "Snail was a small snail," did so only because that opening
  is itself high-probability.)

**Same root cause as the weak dial:** one example per topic, one character of
`green=N` signal — both signals are too sparse for this model size to latch onto.

**Fix (decided — regenerating a fresh 1k corpus to test it):**
- **Repeat each topic across all 5 levels** (~200 unique topics × 5) so every
  topic string is seen 5× *and* the model sees the same topic at green=1 vs
  green=5 — a clean contrastive signal that should teach both topic-honouring
  and the dial at once.
- **Force protagonist diversity:** pass an explicit main-character name per topic
  (rotated across a ~40-name pool) and instruct the story to open on the topic
  before the light appears. Breaks the Mia collapse.
- The `[green=N] topic: …` doc contract is unchanged (the name only shapes the
  story text, never the control line), so `prepare`/`sample`/web-ui are untouched.

## 2026-06-25 — `1k-v2` (200 topics × 5 levels): corpus fixed, model didn't follow

Regenerated a fresh 1k corpus with the redesign (validated first on a 100-story
chunk for $0.29, then the full run for $2.94 — $3.23 this round, ~$6.27 project
total). Same model/training config as v1, only the data changed, so differences
are attributable to the corpus.

**The corpus came out exactly as intended:**
- 200 distinct topics, each at all five levels; themes spread across all 20
  (after fixing `generate_topics` to scale `per_call` to the topic count — a
  large per-call had drained a single theme).
- Protagonist collapse fixed: "Mia" 1681× → 135× (residual is Claude slipping
  the name into dialogue, not protagonist choice).
- Corpus dial steep and clean: avg green mentions **2.27 → 3.20 → 5.22 → 6.47 →
  12.57** across L1..5; green=5 stories get fully swallowed ("More than the
  balloon. More than everything. Green light.").

**But the trained model barely moved:**
- **Obsession still works** — the green light reliably barges into arbitrary
  topics (~3–4 mentions over 480 tokens). Note: at 240 tokens the count looked
  like it *dropped* to ~0.8, but that's a window artifact — the new on-topic
  openings delay the light, so a short window catches the intro and cuts off
  before the obsession. Over a full story it's there. (Arguably an improvement:
  topic first, *then* the light takes over.)
- **Topic honoring: marginally better, still unreliable.** Sometimes a topic word
  surfaces ("snail racing a beetle" → a beetle in a jar; "two bakers" → "woke up
  to bake"), but often not ("a robot" → a rabbit; "a clock" → a firefly).
- **Dial still broken** — model dial (480 tok) is **4.17, 4.08, 3.17, 3.17, 3.17**:
  flat and slightly *inverted*, with levels 3/4/5 byte-identical. The model still
  ignores the lone `green=N` digit.
- **Coherence slightly worse than v1** qualitatively ("He watched his paw out to
  purple"; "They were all by the sat").

**Reading of the result:**
- **val loss is NOT comparable across v1 and v2** — different corpora → different
  val splits. v2's lower 0.521 (@1500) vs v1's 0.639 (@1250) mostly reflects the
  more *repetitive* corpus being easier to predict, not better generation. Same
  overfit-after-min shape (val rises to 0.596 by 3000).
- The data-shape hypothesis (repeat topics + same-protagonist contrast → honour
  topic + obey dial) **did not pan out at this scale.** The corpus signal is now
  clean, so the bottleneck is more fundamental: a ~10M *char-level* model
  conditions only weakly on a short structured prefix (the topic line, and
  especially the 1-char level) sitting dozens–hundreds of characters back. More
  repetition didn't make a 1-char digit louder.
- Plausible cost: fewer unique topics (200 vs 995) with 5× same-protagonist
  repeats may have *reduced* effective diversity and nudged coherence down.

**Levers (NOT run — pausing for a decision):**
- **Make the conditioning physically louder/closer to the generated text**, not
  just more frequent in the corpus: repeat the level tag (`green=5 green=5 …`),
  spell it as words ("OBSESSION: total"), and/or restate it right before the body
  so it's local to where the story is generated.
- **Switch the dial to a few well-separated buckets** (faint / strong / total)
  instead of 5 near-adjacent ones — the model may only resolve coarse contrasts.
- **Move off char-level for the conditioning** (word/BPE tokens make the topic
  and tag carry real token weight) — bigger change, hits the root cause.
- **Accept always-obsessed as the artifact** and drop the fine dial — the
  inescapable green light is the strongest, most reliable behaviour we have.
- If keeping this corpus shape, **dial back the repetition** (2–3 stories/topic)
  to recover topic diversity and coherence.

## 2026-06-25 — `1k-v3` (louder conditioning): the dial works

Tested the top lever from v2 — **make the conditioning louder/closer**, not the
corpus bigger. Crucially this was a **$0, fully-controlled A/B**: the story text
is byte-identical to v2; only the control line changed, via `reformat_corpus.py`
(reprocesses `data/raw.txt` in place, re-emitting each doc through the new
`generate.build_prime`). So any behaviour change is attributable to the signal
format alone.

**New format** (was a single quiet `[green=N] topic: …`):
```
[green=5] [green=5] [green=5] obsession=total
topic: <topic>
<story>
```
The tag is repeated 3× and a distinct word per level is added
(`faint/soft/strong/heavy/total`), so the dial signal carries real
character-mass right above the body. `build_prime` is now the single source of
truth (generate/sample/eval/generate_samples import it; web-ui `buildPrime`
mirrors it char-for-char; verified equal).

**Result — the dial moved for the first time.** Model dial @480 tok:

| level | v2 (flat/inverted) | **v3 (louder)** |
|------|------|------|
| 1 | 4.17 | **1.50** |
| 2 | 4.08 | **1.92** |
| 3 | 3.17 | **1.92** |
| 4 | 3.17 | **3.08** |
| 5 | 3.17 | **3.50** |

Monotonic, ~2.3× ramp L1→L5, and — unlike v1/v2 — **the levels now generate
genuinely different text** under a fixed seed (no more byte-identical L3/L4/L5).
Qualitatively it reads as a real intensity knob: at `faint` the light appears
once near the end; at `strong` it's woven through ("Sam forgot the blanket. Sam
forgot the stars."); at `total` it collapses into the Gatsby beat ("Green light.
Green light."). See `research-docs/samples-1k-v3.md`.

**This confirms the v2 diagnosis:** the bottleneck was signal *loudness/locality*,
not corpus shape or data volume. A lone digit dozens of chars back was too quiet;
a repeated, worded, nearby tag is one the 10M char-model can actually condition
on. val min 0.502 @ step 1500 (same overfit-after-min shape; not comparable
across corpora).

**Still open (unchanged by this lever):**
- **Topic honoring is still unreliable** — "a robot" → a rabbit named Eli, "a
  clock" → "a cloud". The loud tag fixed the *dial* dimension but not topic
  conditioning; that likely needs the same loudness treatment for the topic
  (e.g. restate/repeat topic words) and/or more topic diversity than 200.
- **Coherence is still rough** ("He blue off a lone"; "a little train shipked") —
  small model + char-level + only 200 topics; stopping at the val min (~1500)
  and/or more data would help.

**Next levers (NOT run — pausing):** apply the same loud-signal idea to the topic;
recover coherence (more topics, or stop at val min); or accept this as the
exhibit model — the dial + inescapable light both work now.

## Mixture-of-models corpus — generator swap (2026-06-28)

New direction: stop paying Claude to write the corpus. Generate it from a
**mixture of local open models** via LM Studio (`generate_mixture.py`, engine in
`tools/synthgen/`) — free, "infinite", and distributionally diverse (different
lineages = different voices). Same task, same load-bearing `[green=N]×3
obsession=<word>` contract; only the writers change. Five models calibrated on
the gatsby task (Round 1 calibration): **qwen3.6 dropped** (5–10× slower, verbose);
kept granite-4.1-8b (IBM), gemma-4-26b (Google), olmo-3-7b (AllenAI),
ministral-3-8b (Mistral). Stories are cleaned to gatsby's register (markdown
emphasis stripped, per-sentence newlines folded, unicode→ASCII) — char vocab 73,
all clean ASCII.

### `mix-1k-r1` — blend granite .40 / gemma .30 / olmo .15 / ministral .15

1000 stories (200 topics × 5), 763k chars (note: **34% smaller than the Claude
1k**, ~1.15M — the local models write terser). $0. val min **0.627 @ step 1250**
(overfits after; not comparable across corpora). Each topic's five levels written
by one model (clean within-topic dial); models rotate across topics.

**Result — worse than Claude 1k-v3.** Obsession ✅ (green light barges in every
sample). But **dial flat/broken** (model green mentions/level
`1.67/1.67/1.83/2.00/1.42` — L5 the *lowest*), **coherence rough** (non-words:
"pomered", "bkink"), **topic-honoring poor** ("a robot" → dragons/bees/no robot).

**Diagnosis — the blend, not the pipeline.** The *corpus* dial is strong and
monotonic (green-light/level `2.31 → 3.32 → 4.57 → 5.98 → 8.18`), so the data
modulates fine; the *model* failed to learn it. Per-model corpus dial:

| model | L1 | L2 | L3 | L4 | L5 | |
|------|----|----|----|----|----|--|
| gemma | 4.8 | 6.1 | 8.3 | 12.2 | 16.7 | wide ✅ |
| olmo | 1.1 | 2.9 | 3.7 | 4.9 | 6.5 | clean ✅ |
| ministral | 0.8 | 1.5 | 2.3 | 3.4 | 4.9 | clean ✅ |
| **granite** | 1.4 | 2.1 | 2.9 | **2.7** | 3.7 | **flat ❌** |

**granite — the 40% backbone — has the weakest, near-flat dial.** So 40% of the
corpus teaches "the control line barely predicts intensity." Compounded by the
corpus being 34% smaller (overfits by 1250, before conditioning is learned), the
dial collapses. Round 1 weighted granite for throughput + clean topic-honoring,
but that's exactly the model that *kills the dial*.

**Next — `mix-2k-r2`:** (a) rebalance off granite → **olmo .30 / ministral .30 /
gemma .20 / granite .20** (lean on the fast clean-dial models + gemma's range);
(b) scale to **2000 stories** (~1.4M chars > Claude 1k) so it trains past the
1250 overfit point and learns the conditioning. Round-1 artifacts frozen in
`research/round1_1k/`.

### `mix-2k-r2` — rebalanced + scaled → **released as `gatsby-nanogpt-2`**

2000 stories (400 topics × 5), **1.53M chars** (now *larger* than the Claude 1k),
blend olmo .30 / ministral .30 / gemma .20 / granite .20. $0. Corpus dial clean
monotonic (1.9 → 2.8 → 3.9 → 5.3 → 7.5). vocab 80 (7 stray numerals/symbols,
9 occurrences total — negligible).

**Both fixes landed.** Val kept descending **past step 1250** (where r1 bottomed
and overfit) to a minimum **0.622 @ step 2000** — 60% deeper training, train/val
gap 0.16 vs r1's 0.41 at the same step. The 2× diverse data bought the depth that
let it learn the conditioning.

**Dial recovered** (@480 tok, where the swallow manifests — 240 tok undercounts):

| level | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|------|----|----|----|----|----|
| r1 (flat) | 1.7 | 1.7 | 1.8 | 2.0 | 1.4 |
| **r2** | **3.7** | 4.8 | 4.7 | 4.5 | **6.1** |

Endpoints separate (L1→L5, a working knob); middle (L2–L4) compressed. Obsession
reliable. Coherence + topic-honoring still rough — same baseline-level weaknesses
as v1/Claude (inherent to a 10.7M char-model on a few hundred topics), not the
target of this round.

**Verdict:** a 4-model local mixture matches the paid baseline's behaviour at $0.
The finding is that **the blend is a designed object** — granite's flat per-model
dial broke r1, rebalancing off it fixed r2. Released as `gatsby-nanogpt-2`;
written up in [Experiment 04](../../../research-docs/reports/mixture-of-models.md).
The dial's mid-compression (gemma carries the range, capped at 20% for speed) is
the open lever for a future gemma-heavy round.
